About the Institute

About ISI Bangalore

The Indian Statistical Institute (ISI), founded in 1931 by Professor Prasanta Chandra Mahalanobis, is one of the world's oldest and most prestigious institutions dedicated to statistics, mathematics, and data science. Recognised as an Institution of National Importance by the Government of India through an Act of Parliament (1959), ISI has shaped the analytical foundations of modern India from national economic planning to cutting-edge research in machine learning, statistical inference and stochastic processes.

The Bangalore Centre, established in 1978, extends ISI's legacy into one of India's most vibrant technology and research ecosystems. Situated in the heart of India's Silicon Valley, the centre maintains active research groups in Theoretical and Applied Statistics, Mathematics, Data science and Quality Management sciences.

Global Recognition

ISI degrees are recognised worldwide. Alumni lead data science teams at major technology firms, financial institutions, and research organisations across the globe.

National Entrance Examination

Admission to ISI programmes is conducted through a rigorous national-level selection process, ensuring that only the most analytically gifted students are admitted. For the BSDS program, students are selected from across India based on merit lists derived from national examinations: JEE Main and CUET. This provides a direct opportunity for students appearing in these exams nationwide to compete for admission. The process emphasizes strong mathematical reasoning, statistical intuition, and problem-solving ability, and hence attracting and identifying top talent from all parts of the country.

The Programme

The BSDS Programme

The Bachelor of Statistical Data Science (Honours) is a four-year undergraduate programme designed to train students into industry ready data scientists capable of transforming raw data into reliable insights, building predictive models, and communicating results effectively.

Flexible exit options: Students may exit after 3 years with a Bachelor’s degree, complete 4 years to earn the BSDS (Honours) degree, or go on with a 5th year for an Integrated Master’s degree, allowing students and companies to interact with each other at different levels.

Programme Objective

To make graduates with deep foundations in Mathematics, Statistics, and Computing equipping them with expertise in statistical modelling, machine learning, optimization, data management, and real life problem solving for modern data-driven industries.

Core Domains

Statistics & Probability

From data exploration and inference to Bayesian methods, time series, multivariate analysis, and advanced resampling students develop strong real life based statistical skills.

Mathematics

Four semesters of rigorous mathematics covering linear algebra, real analysis, graph theory, topology and vector calculus building the quantitative backbone for advanced modelling.

Computing & Data Engineering

Data structures, algorithms, database management, R & Python programming, GPU computing, and distributed systems ensuring computational rigor alongside statistical depth.

Interdisciplinary Electives

Causal Inference, Actuarial Statistics, Survival Analysis, Clinical Trials, Statistical Physics, Genetics & Bioinformatics, Statistical Finance broadening analytical perspectives across real-world domains.

Experiential Focus

The programme combines rigorous theory with real world learning through data science projects, industry internships, and project work involving modern tools used in data-driven decision-making across sectors.

Industry Relevance

Graduates are prepared for professional roles in Data Science, Quantitative Analytics, Machine Learning Engineering, and Research with the rare blend of statistical depth and computational proficiency that top employers seek.

Skills & Roles

Skills & Competencies

BSDS graduates are uniquely positioned at the intersection of statistical theory and computational practice. Their skill set spans the full data science pipeline from formulating statistical hypotheses to deploying machine learning models at scale.

Statistical Modelling & Inference

Parametric & non-parametric methods, Bayesian analysis, hypothesis testing, regression, time series forecasting, multivariate analysis, and experimental design.

Machine Learning & AI

Supervised & unsupervised learning, deep learning (CNNs, RNNs, transformers), ensemble methods, model selection, regularisation, and neural architecture design.

Data Engineering & Computing

Database systems, distributed & parallel computing, GPU programming, algorithm design, data pipelines, and scalable data processing architectures.

Optimization & Quantitative Analysis

Linear & nonlinear optimization, stochastic processes, numerical methods, and quantitative strategies for finance and risk analysis.
